About this service

Help us to improve the NHS by acting as our ‘eyes and ears’, tell us what we are doing well and what we need to improve on, so that we can develop the best possible services. We want to ensure your voice is heard, listened to and acted upon.

The service is simple, there are no gimmicks. We connect you quickly to people in the NHS who want to hear what you have to say. You can do this online or text us on 61114.

Note, this is a pilot stage of the on-line service and is being tested in London and parts of the North East of England. We would welcome any comments or suggestions for improvements before we share it with NHS users across the country.

Here’s how it works:-

Share your experience

Share your experience - tell us about when you have experienced good care and when you have not. We also want to hear about your suggestions for what we can do to improve.

Ask a question

Receive an online answer to your questions about NHS services. To make it easier we have also included some quick links to the most commonly asked questions.

Report a problem

If you have a problem that needs resolving, or you want to report something that needs our attention, submit it here and we will connect you directly with people at the NHS service who are best placed to help.

See how local services are responding in real time

Our live map and summary tables show how NHS providers are responding to people in real time.

Together we can build a better NHS

Why Have We Developed This Service?

The NHS Constitution explains how the NHS is accountable to you. It tells you how we will ensure NHS health and care services in England are delivered to the highest quality standard.

This new online service is part of our commitment. It gives you a direct route to find out information, ask us questions, get problems resolved and let us know what you think of the services you experience.

By listening to you we can make sure that NHS services are based on the needs of patients and their families. Be our eyes and ears and help us build a better NHS.

What happens next?

We will send your problem report to the NHS provider immediately. They will then contact you directly to try to get it resolved.

You can expect to receive an acknowledgement from the NHS provider. The NHS provider will agree with you the expected time to get your problem resolved. If your problem requires a more detailed response or formal investigation, the NHS provider will work with you to ensure this happens.

If you have chosen to make the details of your problem visible to the public (to help others who may be experiencing something similar) this will appear a short while later after passing through our moderation process. You can use your reference number to track the progress of your problem.

The NHS provider will change the status of your problem from 'open' to 'in progress' when they make contact with you. When your problem has been resolved the status will be changed to 'closed'. This removes it from the live map but it will still be visible on the NHS provider summary.

What does the status means?

Open

This means your problem has been forwarded to the NHS provider and appears on the live map and the provider summary.

In-Progress

This means the NHS provider has received your problem and will contact you directly to work out how best to resolve it.

Closed

This means your problem has been resolved and marked as 'closed' by the NHS provider. It will no longer appear on the live map but will still be visible on the provider summary.

You will receive an email asking you to complete a short survey 3 weeks after posting your problem to find out if you feel your problem was resolved. The results of this survey will be published on this site.

Live map

You can look up your local NHS provider on our live map and see what issues are being responded to in real time.

The map shows the number of problems that have been submitted. A blue dot on the map marks the location of the NHS provider. The darker the shade of blue indicates the higher the number of problems that are currently 'open' or 'in progress'.

Type in the postcode or the name of the NHS provider to zoom in on a specific area of the map.

Hover over a dot to see an 'at a glance' summary.

Click on the dot to go to the NHS provider summary where your can track your problem using your booking reference number and also see what others have reported and how the NHS is responding.

If you cannot see the NHS provider you are looking for, it may be that they are not currently participating in the pilot service.

Note: A higher number of problems may not necessarily indicate a poorly performing NHS provider - it could be that they are actively promoting this feedback service. Check out their summary page to see how they are doing.

What can I do if my problem is not resolved?

Our aim is to try to get problems resolved locally with your NHS provider as far as we can. If you are not satisfied with the final response you received about your problem from the local service provider and feel that all efforts for a local resolution have been exhausted, you may wish to take it further by contacting the Health Service Ombudsman.

The Health Service Ombudsman’s role is to investigate complaints that individuals have been treated unfairly or received poor service from the NHS in England. You can contact them to find out how they can help by:

  • Visiting their website
  • Calling 0345 015 4033 (staff are available Monday to Friday from 8.30am to 5.30pm) (if you are deaf or have problems using a standard telephone please call 0300 061 4298 using a textphone)
  • Email: phso.enquiries@ombudsman.org.uk
  • Text 'call back' with your name and mobile number to 07624 813 005 and they will call you back

Or you can write to the Health Service Ombudsman at:

Millbank Tower
Millbank
London SW1P 4QP

Please remember: The Ombudsman will normally only take on a complaint after you have first tried to resolve the issues with the NHS provider involved and have received a final response from them.

You will also need to tell them how you have been affected by the issues you are complaining about (or, if you are complaining on behalf of somebody else, how they have been affected).
