About this service

Help us to improve the NHS by acting as our ‘eyes and ears’, tell us what we are doing well and what we need to improve on, so that we can develop the best possible services. We want to ensure your voice is heard, listened to and acted upon.

The service is simple, there are no gimmicks. We connect you quickly to people in the NHS who want to hear what you have to say. You can do this online or text us on 61114.

Note, this is a pilot stage of the on-line service and is being tested in London and parts of the North East of England. We would welcome any comments or suggestions for improvements before we share it with NHS users across the country. Email us your thoughts at {{ settings.FEEDBACK_EMAIL_ADDRESS }}.

Here’s how it works:-

Share your experience

Share your experience - tell us about when you have experienced good care and when you have not. We also want to hear about your suggestions for what we can do to improve.

Ask a question

Report a problem

If you have a problem that needs resolving, or you want to report something that needs our attention, submit it here and we will connect you directly with people at the NHS service who are best placed to help.

See how local services are responding in real time

Our live map and summary tables show how NHS providers are responding to people in real time.

Together we can build a better NHS

Why Have We Developed This Service?

The NHS Constitution explains how the NHS is accountable to you. It tells you how we will ensure NHS health and care services in England are delivered to the highest quality standard.

This new online service is part of our commitment. It gives you a direct route to find out information, ask us questions, get problems resolved and let us know what you think of the services you experience.

By listening to you we can make sure that NHS services are based on the needs of patients and their families. Be our eyes and ears and help us build a better NHS.

What happens next?

We will send your problem report to the NHS provider immediately. They will then contact you directly to try to get it resolved.

You can expect to receive an acknowledgement from the NHS provider. The NHS provider will agree with you the expected time to get your problem resolved. If your problem requires a more detailed response or formal investigation, the NHS provider will work with you to ensure this happens.

If you have chosen to make the details of your problem visible to the public (to help others who may be experiencing something similar) this will appear a short while later after passing through our moderation process. You can use your reference number to track the progress of your problem.

The NHS provider will change the status of your problem from 'open' to 'in progress' when they make contact with you. When your problem has been resolved the status will be changed to 'closed'. This removes it from the live map but it will still be visible on the NHS provider summary.

What does the status means?

Open

This means your problem has been forwarded to the NHS provider and appears on the live map and the provider summary.

In-Progress

This means the NHS provider has received your problem and will contact you directly to work out how best to resolve it.

Closed

This means your problem has been resolved and marked as 'closed' by the NHS provider. It will no longer appear on the live map but will still be visible on the provider summary.

You will receive an email asking you to complete a short survey 3 weeks after posting your problem to find out if you feel your problem was resolved. The results of this survey will be published on this site.

Live map

You can look up your local NHS provider on our live map and see what issues are being responded to in real time.

The map shows the number of problems that have been submitted. A blue dot on the map marks the location of the NHS provider. The darker the shade of blue indicates the higher the number of problems that are currently 'open' or 'in progress'.

Type in the postcode or the name of the NHS provider to zoom in on a specific area of the map.

Hover over a dot to see an 'at a glance' summary.

Click on the dot to go to the NHS provider summary where your can track your problem using your booking reference number and also see what others have reported and how the NHS is responding.

If you cannot see the NHS provider you are looking for, it may be that they are not currently participating in the pilot service.

Note: A higher number of problems may not necessarily indicate a poorly performing NHS provider - it could be that they are actively promoting this feedback service. Check out their summary page to see how they are doing.

A problem fixed is a positive outcome for you and the NHS.

Provider summaries

Click on 'provider summaries' on the top navigation bar to see more information about problems reported.

You can filter by NHS provider, service/department, category and status. Click on each section to see more detail, including any responses from the NHS provider.

If you have submitted a problem - the category of your problem and the name of the service/department will automatically appear. If you have chosen to make the details of your problem public (to help others who may be experiencing something similar) this will appear a short while later after passing through our moderation process. You can use your booking reference number to track the progress of your problem.

What can I do if my problem is not resolved?

Our aim is to try to get problems resolved locally with your NHS provider as far as we can. If you are not satisfied with the final response you received about your problem from the local service provider and feel that all efforts for a local resolution have been exhausted, you may wish to take it further by contacting the Health Service Ombudsman.

The Health Service Ombudsman’s role is to investigate complaints that individuals have been treated unfairly or received poor service from the NHS in England. You can contact them to find out how they can help by:

  • Visiting their website
  • Calling 0345 015 4033 (staff are available Monday to Friday from 8.30am to 5.30pm) (if you are deaf or have problems using a standard telephone please call 0300 061 4298 using a textphone)
  • Email: phso.enquiries@ombudsman.org.uk
  • Text 'call back' with your name and mobile number to 07624 813 005 and they will call you back

Or you can write to the Health Service Ombudsman at:

Millbank Tower
Millbank
London SW1P 4QP

Please remember: The Ombudsman will normally only take on a complaint after you have first tried to resolve the issues with the NHS provider involved and have received a final response from them.

You will also need to tell them how you have been affected by the issues you are complaining about (or, if you are complaining on behalf of somebody else, how they have been affected).

Comments policy

Contributions to Care Connect may be moderated, that is, checked to make sure they don’t break our rules. You will need to keep your contributions constructive, relevant and civil. The following rules and guidelines are to protect both you and the NHS, and our moderators will remove contributions that break these rules.

Commenting on NHS services (including hospitals, GP practices and dentists)

The Care Connect service launches initially as a pilot allowing the public to raise problems, ask questions and share their experiences of NHS service by reviewing and rating services. The pilot will go-live with hospitals in the first instance. GPs will follow at a later date.

Share Your Experience - The service you receive from the NHS will never be adversely affected by your feedback on hospitals, GP surgeries, dental practices or any other health provider and you can comment anonymously if you wish. You should respect the anonymity of other patients and staff at the service you are commenting on by not using their names.

Problems - The service you receive from the NHS will never be adversely affected by the problems you raise regarding hospitals, GP surgeries or any other health provider. You must provide a contact name when raising a problem. This is to allow a provider to fully investigate your problem. Your contact details will never be published publicly on the Care Connect site.

Question – The question service is anonymous and any questions raised are answered by Care Connect case handlers.

The “Patient feedback” feature for each hospital, GP and dental practice in England is there for you to comment on your, or a friend’s or family member’s, hospital, general practice, dental or other care service. This is not a formal complaint procedure. If you wish to make a complaint you should read our guide to making a complaint (http://www.nhs.uk/choiceinthenhs/rightsandpledges/complaints/pages/nhscomplaints.aspx).

NHS trusts and the service providers are not obliged to read the comments, though many do. If you want a reply to your feedback you should contact the provider of the NHS service either directly or through the local Patient Advice and Liaison Service (PALS), listed on the page of the hospital, GP practice, dental practice or other service you have used.

Giving your feedback on an NHS service does not require registration with Care Connect but as a minimum you will need to provide a valid email address so that we can contact you if there are any problems.

Care Connect is not suitable for making general political points about NHS services. Feedback from members of staff will not be accepted. Comments must be about events within the past 2 years. Once a previously accepted comment is more than 2 years old, we will indicate this next to the comment.

Complaining about another person’s comment

Share Your Experience - If you think that someone’s contribution to the website breaks our rules you can alert one of our moderators by clicking the links provided to report a comment. Reporting a comment will not automatically remove it but will ensure we review it as soon as possible. The moderators will then decide whether to remove it permanently or reinstate it.

Problems - If you think that someone’s contribution to the website breaks our rules you can alert one of our moderators by emailing ccfeedback@nhs.net to report a comment. Reporting a comment will not automatically remove it but will ensure we review it as soon as possible. The moderators will then decide whether to remove it permanently or reinstate it.

Relevance, civility and decency

Relevance: comments must be relevant to the Organisation they are to appear on.

Be civil: please ensure that your contributions are respectful of others. We will remove contributions that are unlawful, harassing, abusive, threatening, obscene, sexually suggestive, racist, homophobic or sexist or that incite or promote hatred of any group of individual.

Writing in capitals: do not write in capital letters – on the internet this is regarded as "shouting" and many people are offended by it.

Be informative: ensure that your comment has enough information to describe your experience, and if you are commenting on a hospital, GP practice, dental practice or community NHS Service, provide enough information to assist someone else in making decisions about the service.

Abusive language: comments containing swearing will be removed. People of all ages use Care Connect and language should be suitable for them.

English: all comments on the website must be made in English. Comments in other languages will be removed.

Stereotypes: comments that stereotype sections of society will be removed.

Multiple posts: duplicate comments or repeats will be removed.
