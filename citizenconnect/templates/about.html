About this service

We want to make it easier for you to share your opinions and concerns about the NHS services you use, and in the process, your insights will help us to build a better NHS.

On this site, you can report any problems you may have with an NHS service, ask us a question and leave ratings and reviews. You can also see what other people have said about your local services, and view in real time how they are responding to other people's problems.

It is in a pilot phase, so at present this service is live for hospitals and community NHS services in some parts of London and the North East. This will expand quickly to cover a wider geographical area and a bigger range of services in the NHS, public health (should we include them) and social care. We will also be adding the option to contact the service by telephone, text messaging and via apps.

Submitting Problems

The idea of this site is to give you a single place for you to submit problems, suggestions, and things you think the NHS should know about. We take care of making sure that what you have said is sent to the people who can help you best. This is what you do…

Step 1

Use the search to find the NHS service you wish to send your problem to. You then complete a simple form to give us details of your problem.

Step 2

Some people choose to have the details of their problem published on this site because it helps others who may have a similar problem. Choosing to publish the problem on this site makes it easier to track.

You will be presented with three options:

  • Keep the issue private between you and the NHS organisation
  • Publish your problem anonymously
  • Publish your problem and give your name

If you aren't sure, the private option is the default. If you do choose to have your issue published on this site the content of the message will have to pass through a moderation process before it appears.

Step 3

When you press submit, the information in your form is immediately sent directly to your chosen service for them to respond. They will get in touch with you directly using the information you have given. (You are required to provide your name and also either your email address or telephone number for this reason.)

Step 4

You will be given a reference number on a confirmation page. Please make a note of this number as you can use it to track the progress of your problem.

Step 5

The NHS service will acknowledge they have received your problem and will change its online status to "In Progress". They should contact you by email or telephone (depending on the preference you give) to agree a solution. You should get a response within X working days.

Step 6

Once your problem has been resolved the NHS service will change the status to "Closed".

If you are not satisfied with the final outcome, or you do not get a response, you can re-log your problem, but this time select the "This relates to a previous problem" option in the form and include your previous reference number if you have it and provide details.

The fact that you are re-logging a problem will mean it is escalated, to be investigated at a higher level. Who takes on this investigation will depend on which NHS service the problem relates to but it will either be the Customer Contact Centre or your local Clinical Commissioning Group (CCG). You will be informed who is investigating your problem and you will still be able to track its progress.

Making Complaints

All problems posted through these pages are treated as important but if you feel that your problem is serious and that you wish to make a formal complaint instead, here's what you need to do:

Contact the NHS England Customer Contact Centre directly:

By Post

NHS Commissioning Board
PO Box 16728
Redditch
B97 9PT

By telephone

0300 311 22 33 (Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm, excluding English Bank Holidays)

By Email

nhscommissioningboard@hscic.gov.uk

If after you have made your complaint you are still unhappy with how it was dealt with you can take your complaint further with the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman or by telephone at 0345 015 4033.

Reviews and Ratings

In the same way that you can leave online reviews about experiences such as holidays, hotels and restaurants, and give them star ratings, you can now review and rate your recent NHS experiences. Tell NHS services what they are good at and also what they can do to improve. Reviews are published on this site and will help other people when making decisions about where to go for their healthcare.

Our Live Interactive Maps

You can look up your local NHS service on our interactive maps and see a live view of how they are performing in real time. Whenever anybody uses this service to post a problem it is instantly sent to the service provider, who will get in touch with you and help find a resolution. The problem is given a category and is flagged on the interactive map. When you post a problem you have the option to have the actual content of your message published on the map as well, although you can also choose to remain completely anonymous. You can track how quickly the service is responding, and can also read some of the details on the kind of issues other people have.

Why Have We Developed This Service?

The NHS is committed to delivering services of the highest standards, based on what patients, like you and your family need. To achieve, this it has to be accountable to the public and make it easy for you to be heard when you have a complaint or suggestion to make. This is a fundamental part of the NHS Constitution, our promise to you, which you can read about here [link].

This service will make the NHS better for everyone. You benefit because you will find it easier to get the information you need from just one point of contact. Health and care services benefit too, because they get a chance to have a conversation with you. They can find what they're good at and what they need to do to improve, driving up standards in NHS health and care services across England.

How Can You Help Us Develop This Service?

This is an ambitious project that can really change the way the NHS and care services interact with the public but for it to work we want people to be engaging with us. You can help us by contributing and leaving reviews about your recent NHS experience, good or bad. If you have any problems that need solving, or any questions relating to NHS or care services in your area, please post them through these pages on this site.

Finally, you can help us by telling us what you think of this service. It is still in pilot stage and we will welcome any suggestions and thoughts you may have on how we can make this better and easier for you to use. You can contact us.... [add contact info].
